The Beginning (1950s)

Some of the first computerized video games were Bertie The Brain,the Tic-Tac-Toe game, and Turochamp, The chess game. The first games to use physics were Tennis for Two and Spacewar!. Created in 1947, the Cathode Ray Amusement Device became the first interactive electronic game and the first game to use an electronic dispaly.

Video Games at Home (1960-1983)

Through the 60's, 70's, and into the 80's, many events happened in video game history. Computer Space was created, an arcade version of Pong was released, and Breakout was also created.

The Rise of Games in the East (1985-1995)

  1. Second and third generation consoles emerged. The 8-bit third generation consoles emerged between 1983 and 1995
  2. Arcade games, which had lost popularity in North America due to the rise of home gaming, began to gain popularity in the East.
  3. Nintendo released the Game Boy in 1989. This was the first handheld console since the failed Microvision ten years ago.

Tech Company Takeover (1995-2010)

  1. Sega released the Sega Dreamcast in 1998. It was initially successful due to it being the first console with intenet support and online play, but sales quickly declined.
  2. Sony's PlayStation 2 was released in 2000 and featured DVD based game discs with a capacity of 4.7GB, incresed graphics abilities, four-player connection, and an available ethernet adapter. This console was very successful during it's generation.
  3. Nintendo released the Gamecube in 2001. This became the company's first optical disc-based console.

Today in Gaming (2010-Now)

  1. 43% of American adults say that they often or sometimes play video games on a variety of devices.
  2. Puzzle and strategy games are among the most popular genres.
  3. Gaming is most popular among teenage boys
  4. 29% of gamers are under 8, 32% are between 18-35 years old, and 39% are 36 and above
  5. 59% of Americans play video games and 42% play three or more hours a week
